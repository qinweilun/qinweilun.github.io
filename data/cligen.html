Gridded Input Parameters for a Stochastic Weather Generator, CLIGEN, in 10-km resolution of China

CLIGEN is a stochastic weather generator which was released in 1995 initially accompanied by the process-based soil erosion model Water Erosion Prediction Project (WEPP) by the United States Department of Agriculture. Detailed information about CLIGEN, and the programs and source code can be obtained from the following website:
https://www.ars.usda.gov/midwest-area/west-lafayette-in/national-soil-erosion-research/docs/wepp/cligen/

CLIGEN can simulate a series of long-term climate data. Of the ten CLIGEN-simulated weather elements, seven, namely daily maximum and minimum temperature, daily precipitation, duration, time to peak intensity (tp), the ratio of the peak intensity to the average intensity (ip), and daily solar radiation, are all that are required for predicting hydrological processes, soil erosion, and bio-production, and are considered to meet the minimum data requirements for WEPP if modeling wind-induced snow drift is not needed.

This dataset represents a gridded input parameters of CLIGEN for the mainland China, and each input file contains thirteen groups of input parameters related to simulation of temperature, solar radiation and precipitation parameters. These parameters were firstly calculated based on long-term of observed data, including daily temperature, daily precipitation and hourly precipitation collected from 2405 stations and daily solar radiation collected from 130 stations in China), and then regionalized to the mainland China in 10-km resolution by universal Kriging. The quality of interpolated parameters has been fully assessed using cross-validation.
